Biography

Gruden's Plan entered the league in 2017 and had a chip on their shoulder from the beginning. However, they quickly realized that they may be a little in over their heads as they finished the season in 6th place thanks in large part to the league's most infamous trade rape where Gruden's Plan sent over Tom Brady and LeSean McCoy to The Dictator in exchange for Marcus Mariota and Ty Montgomery. This trade was so atrocious that it would be mocked on national television by the former NFL Fantasy Analyst Michael Fabiano.

After realizing that this league is tougher than they first thought, Gruden's Plan brought in former North Point High School kicker Evan Patterson as his co-manager for the 2018 season, making him the first ever co-manager in league history. They would also adopt the new name "Gruden's Plan" as a nod to the owner's favorite NFL coach Jon Gruden. The addition of co-manager, a new team name, and a draft that he roster-baited to only inflated the hype and the confidence of Gruden's Plan. However, the shock of reality would hit, and they would finish the season with a mere 2 wins in last place. This poor performance made many skeptical of Gruden's Plan, as even The Red Army declared that "Gruden has no plan." Following this disappointing season, Gruden's Plan and Evan agreed to "mutually part ways" and Gruden's Plan went on a search for a new co-manager for the 2019 season.

A few weeks prior to the 2019 draft, Gruden's Plan announced that they will be bringing on unstable friend Jacob Kernster as their new co-manager. However, on the day of the draft, Gruden's Plan showed up without Jacob and announced that he had been let go for reasons still unknown. Gruden's Plan would go into the 2019 season without a co-manager and would begin the season 3-1 and sat in first place. However, their team soon began to fall apart and would even lose the last 7 games of the season to finish the year in 8th place.

Knowing that he could not find success on his own, Gruden's Plan went in search of a new co-manager. That season, The Washington Buttkickers and owner Logan Handwork had been gracefully removed from the league for lack of participation. Gruden's Plan would then immediately offer Mr. Handwork a co-manager position that he graciously accepted. This year Gruden's Plan seemed to turn things around. Firstly, they completed a major trade with the Maryland Marijuanas that would be named the 2020 Trade Rape of the year, this time in Gruden's Plan's favor. In this trade they received Patrick Mahomes, Alvin Kamara, DeSean Jackson, and a 1st round Waiver Wire Draft pick for Todd Gurley, Adam Thielen, Zach Ertz, and Drew Brees. Gruden's Plan continued to make smart trades as they traded away Derrick Henry and a recently injured Austin Ekeler over to Babylon for DeAndre Hopkins, Travis Kelce, and Devin Singletary. With the help of these additions Gruden's Plan would set some league records including the most points in a week at 201.80 points and the most points by player in a week with Alvin Kamara's championship week 54.70 points. All this led Gruden's Plan to their first championship in franchise history. Now was this season just a fluke? Or might Gruden now actually have a plan? Only time will tell.
