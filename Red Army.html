Biography

The Red Army would enter the league in its inaugural season in 2013 and is only one of two teams to have participated in every season since the league's inception. In their first year, The Red Army struggled to muster up consistent wins and ended the regular season at 5-8 in 5th place. They would take on the Huntingtown Hurricanes in the play-in game for the chance to clinch a playoff spot. The Red Army would roll past the Huntingtown Hurricanes and would punch their ticket to their first playoff appearance. They now had to take on Big Ounce and they would suffer a narrow defeat that would knock them into the consolation game and they ended the year in fourth place.

In the 2014 season, The Red Army sought to avenge their playoff shortcomings. They would finish the year with a similar record as last year at 6-8. However, the league now had eight members and this record was only good enough for 5th place, just missing the playoffs. The Red Army would finish the year in 5th place with an even 8-8 record.

In the 2015 season, The Red Army would have its best season to date. They would finish the regular season with an 8-6 record. However, once again this record was not good enough to make the playoffs as they would be set in 5th place. They would go on to win back-to-back Toes Bowls and finish the year in 5th place at 10-6.

In the 2016 season, The Red Army desired to break out of this mediocre rut and return to the playoffs for the first time in three years. However, this dream would be cut short very early on as they would only muster 2 wins in the first eight weeks of the season. But a late year resurgence allowed The Red Army to come back and finish the regular season at 6-8, again in 5th place. Then in the Toes Bowl, The Red Army would dominate once again and win for the third straight season.

Entering the 2017 season, The Red Army was sick of being a joke in this league after 5 straight years of just missing out on the playoffs. This chip on their shoulder pushed them to their best finish in franchise history with a 9-5 record, which was good enough to finally clinch the playoffs in second place. The Red Army was now back in the playoffs for the first time since 2013. They would have the tall task of taking on the Cookie Charizards in search of their first playoff win. In this matchup, The Red Army would fail to perform and lose by more than 50 points. They ended the year in 4th place with a 9-7 record.

Now in the 2018 season, The Red Army had finally found the recipe for the playoffs a year prior and had planned to take a similar approach in 2018. However, this recipe seemed flawed as they would only have one win after the first six weeks of the season. They would end the season with their worst year yet at 4-10 in 8th place. Since they finished in 8th place, they would get to compete in the Toes Bowl bracket once again, where they are 6-0 in Toes Bowl matches with a record 3 Toes Bowl championships. Here they would make it to the Toes Bowl championship game once again but would lose to The Hurts Helicopter and they would finish this disappointing year in 6th place with a pitiful 5-11 record.

In the 2019 season, The Red Army made a dramtic name change and released an iconic video to go along with it. In this video, The Red Army bashed the RFL and its commissioner for the removal of Tyler Ulmschneider and his Cookie Charizards. Furthermore, he acknowledged that Tyler himself is the true fantasy god and would change his own team name to "Cookie Charizards" to honor him. In the video he also uttered the infamous line "Gruden has no plan," a bash at Michael Boswell's Gruden's Plan. Despite his belief in this new God, he would start the season with the worst record of his career with 1 win after 7 weeks. He would finish the season at 5-9 in 9th place and would compete against The Hurts Helicopter in the first ever Panzerkampfwagen Bowl. After the grueling two week battle, The Red Army would come out on top and avoid the league's first punishment. So after this pitiful season, The Red Army would realize that Tyler was a false idol and abandon him.

Leading up to the 2020 season, Brian would reject Tyler but his skepticism for the league and its commissioner only grew. This led to another video that acted as a manifesto. Here The Red Army called for the members of the league to unite and reject the league's commissioner Jeremy Harley. He paints the commissioner as a fascist leader that acts in his own self-interest and cares not about the common league member. He also calls for the league to adopt a communist governing structure where no person has any more power over another. To that effect, he also acknowledged the "father of communism" Karl Marx as his new fantasy God and he would adopt the new team name "The Red Army". The Red Army would start the season very hot as they would win 3 out of their first 4 games. This was all on the back of very well drafted team, as they would receive the best draft award, as well as some fortunate injuries. Marlon Mack would tear his achilles in week 1 allowing The Red Army's Jonathan Taylor to take over the backfield as well as Nick Chubb's MCL sprain that allowed The Red Army's Kareem Hunt to see an increased workload. However, this good luck would soon turn sour as they would only win 2 of their remaining 10 games. This would give them to finish the regular season at 5-9 in 9th place. This would mean The Red Army would enter back-to-back Panzerkamfwagen Bowls, this time facing off against his rival The Dictator. This time The Red Army would be defeated and would finish the year in last place. This poor finish forced him to endure a cruel punishment put on by the other league members as each member was able to throw a pie in the face of The Red Army's owner, Brian Levesque. We have not heard any further word since The Red Army suffered this embarrassing punishment.
